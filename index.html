Background


Image Captioning is a task in the machine learning field that receives images as input and performs description, classification, recognition, etc. according to their characteristics. Radiology Report Generation is a field that applies this Image Captioning task to CXR (Chest X-ray) in the medical domain. The Radiology Report Generation Task is the process of converting radiographs into text and obtaining detailed findings and impressions. However, unlike Image Captioning, this task relies heavily on domain knowledge because disease, symptoms, causes, etc. must be expressed in connection with the report. For this reason, the Radiology Report Generation Task utilizes additional data other than image-text data to generate a report that accurately describes the lesion.

Additionally, the Radiology Report Generation Task has recently been attracting attention as a computer-based diagnosis that reduces the workload of radiologists. However, reports from actual clinical settings include the causes and consequences of the condition to counteract inconsistencies and errors. Therefore, an accurate report applicable in clinical practice requires including relevant information such as symptoms, causes, etc. about the condition.

This dataset is data collected from radiology residents in Korea. The dataset consists of 5 questions and answers, and questions 1 to 4 are the interpretation results of chest radiographs and include disease and anatomical information. Question 5 is a question extracted from the doctor qualification examination workbook and is a checklist of the causes of the condition. This dataset covers approximately 95% of MIMIC-CXR and is linked to the Korean healthcare terminology standard. The dataset consists of 200 pairs of chest radiology images, radiology reports, and questions and answers, as shown in the picture.

Effect, (Q1) Setting up a diagnostic hypothesis

Please write the patient’s abnormal finding : Pneumothorax

Effect, (Q2~3) Additional information about diagnosis

Select from the anatomical location for your abnormal finding : Right Pleural
Select thoracic spine level for abnormal finding : T8~T11

Effect, (Q4) Final diagnosis

Please select up to 3 words that can be included in the reading : Pneumothorax

Causal, (Q5) Reason for diagnosis

Compare the apical, upper, middle and lower zones in turn. Are they symmetrical? Pneumothorax


The picture is a composition of the final dataset showing pairs of chest radiographs and Q&A. Q&A 1 is the doctor's hypothesis setting stage regarding chest radiographs and is an intuitive judgment. Questions 2 and 3 provide additional information about the diagnosis, including anatomical location and spinal level. Q&A 4 is the final diagnosis, which is the doctor's final judgment on the chest radiograph. The last question and answer 5 is the basis for the judgment.

In the case of photographs, the reason for determining Pneummothorax in radiological images was checked by symmetrical comparison. In particular, according to the right pleural in Q&A 2, the symmetry of the lower zone among the apical, upper, middle and lower zones is the basis for diagnosis.
